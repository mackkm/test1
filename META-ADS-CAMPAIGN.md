# Pawlettes — Meta Traffic Ad Campaign ($30 Budget)

Everything you need to launch a traffic ad driving clicks to **pawlette.shop**.
Copy is written to Meta's character limits and ready to paste into Ads Manager.

> **Objective:** Traffic (link clicks to your store)
> **Total budget:** $30
> **You publish it** — Meta requires your logged-in account, payment method,
> and ad review. This doc gives you the copy + exact steps so it's a paste-and-go.

---

## Budget strategy for $30

With $30, don't spread thin. Two sensible options:

| Option | Daily budget | Duration | Best for |
|--------|-------------|----------|----------|
| **A — Quick read (recommended)** | $10/day | 3 days | Fast signal on which audience/creative clicks |
| **B — Steady test** | $6/day | 5 days | Smoother delivery, more days of data |

Either way, keep it to **one ad set, 1–2 ads** so the budget isn't split.
Meta needs ~$5–10/day minimum to exit "learning" and deliver sensibly.

---

## Recommended structure

```
Campaign  →  Objective: Traffic
   └─ Ad set  →  Audience + budget + placements
        └─ Ad  →  Carousel showing ALL 8 products (one per card)
```

Since you want to feature **all your products**, use a **carousel ad** —
it shows one product per swipeable card, all in a single ad. This keeps your
whole $30 behind one ad (best for a small budget) while still showcasing the
full range. Put the **LED Glow Safety Collar first** as the scroll-stopper,
since its glow is the most eye-catching.

## Audience targeting

**Location:** Your selling region (e.g. United States)
**Age:** 25–55
**Gender:** All
**Detailed targeting (interests) — pick 3–5:**
- Dog
- Dog owner
- Pet store
- Petco / PetSmart / Chewy (brand interests)
- Dog park
- Animal rescue / Dog adoption

**Tip:** Keep estimated audience size in the 500k–2M range (Ads Manager shows
this on the right). Too narrow starves delivery on a small budget.

## Step-by-step: launch in Meta Ads Manager

### 1. Open Ads Manager
- Go to **facebook.com/adsmanager** (or business.facebook.com)
- Make sure you're on the right ad account, with a payment method added
  (**Billing → Payment settings** if not).

### 2. Create the campaign
1. Click **+ Create**
2. Choose objective: **Traffic** → **Continue**
3. Campaign name: `Pawlettes – Traffic – LED Collar`
4. Leave Advantage Campaign Budget **off** (you'll set budget at ad-set level)
5. Click **Next**

### 3. Set up the ad set
1. **Conversion location:** **Website**
2. **Daily budget:** `$10` (Option A) or `$6` (Option B)
3. **Schedule:** start today; set an end date 3 days out (A) or 5 days (B)
   — this hard-caps spend at ~$30.
4. **Audience:** set location, age 25–55, and the dog interests listed above
5. **Placements:** **Advantage+ placements** (recommended) — lets Meta
   optimize across Feed, Stories, Reels for cheapest clicks
6. Click **Next**

### 4. Build the ad (carousel)
1. **Ad name:** `All Products – Carousel`
2. **Identity:** select your Pawlettes Facebook Page
3. **Format:** **Carousel**
4. **Primary text:** paste the shared caption (from the creative section)
5. For **each of the 8 cards**:
   - Upload the matching PNG from `products/png-exports/`
   - Paste that card's **Headline** from the table
   - Set **Website URL:** `https://pawlette.shop`
6. **Call to action:** `Shop Now` (set for all cards)
7. (Optional) Add a final card linking to the full store
8. Check the **Ad preview** on the right — swipe through all cards on Feed + Stories

### 5. Publish
1. Click **Publish**
2. Ad goes into **review** (usually approved within a few hours)
3. Once active, it starts spending toward your daily budget

## After launch — what to watch (first 48h)

Check these columns in Ads Manager:

| Metric | What's good | What it means |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| **CPC (cost per link click)** | < $0.50–$1.00 | Cheaper = ad/audience resonates |
| **CTR (link click-through rate)** | > 1% | People want to click |
| **Link clicks** | the goal | Traffic landing on pawlette.shop |

**Don't panic-edit in the first 24h** — Meta is "learning." If after the full
run CPC is high or CTR is low, swap the primary text (try an alternate hook
above) or tighten the audience, then run again.

## Bonus: install the Meta Pixel (for next time)

Even on a traffic campaign, installing the Pixel now means you can retarget
clickers later with sales ads. In Shopify: **Settings → Apps → Facebook &
Instagram** channel, or **Online Store → Preferences → Facebook Pixel**, and
paste your Pixel ID from Meta Events Manager. Not required for this $30 run,
but worth doing before you scale up.
